‘Babylon": a fresco of Hollywood's bygone golden age

Damien Chazelle's ‘Babylon’ is a grandiose and controversial fresco of 1920s Hollywood, combining decadence and creativity to depict the transition from silent to talking pictures.

‘Babylon’, a film directed by Damien Chazelle and released in 2022, plunged viewers into the Hollywood of the 1920s, at the dawn of talking pictures. This grandiose and decadent fresco depicted an industry in the throes of change, where excess and creativity came together in a frenetic whirlwind.

The plot followed the intertwined destinies of several characters emblematic of this pivotal period: Manny Torres (Diego Calva), a young Mexican dreaming of a career in the cinema, Nellie LaRoy (Margot Robbie), an ambitious starlet, and Jack Conrad (Brad Pitt), a silent film star faced with the arrival of talking pictures.

Chazelle delivered a breathtaking visual work, alternating unbridled party scenes with moments of pure cinematic poetry. The meticulous reconstruction of the film sets and backstage Hollywood transported the viewer to the heart of this effervescent period.

The film brilliantly tackled the themes of ambition, fame and technological change in the film industry. It highlighted the sacrifices, excesses and passion of those who made the films, while underlining the ephemeral nature of Hollywood fame.

The soundtrack, composed by long-time Chazelle collaborator Justin Hurwitz, contributed to the film's spellbinding atmosphere. It skilfully blended jazz from the 1920s with more modern sounds, reflecting the duality between tradition and innovation at the heart of the story.

‘Babylon’ divided critics and audiences, with some praising its audacity and excess, and others criticising its frenetic pace and length. Despite these divided opinions, the film established itself as an ambitious and passionate work, testifying to Chazelle's love of cinema and its history.

In conclusion, Babylon offered a dizzying plunge into the origins of Hollywood, celebrating the magic of cinema while exposing its darkest aspects. It was a vibrant tribute to a bygone era, which also questioned the place of the seventh art in our contemporary society.

Ces films, bien que différents dans leurs approches et leurs époques, partagent un point commun fondamental : ils rendent tous hommage au cinéma. Que ce soit en explorant les coulisses de l'industrie, en célébrant l'âge d'or d'Hollywood, ou en montrant la passion et les sacrifices nécessaires pour réussir dans ce milieu, chacun de ces films offre un regard affectueux et nostalgique sur le septième art. Ils reflètent l'amour de leurs réalisateurs pour le cinéma, tout en explorant ses complexités et ses contradictions.
