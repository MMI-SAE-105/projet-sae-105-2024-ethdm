Soft power :

Soft power refers to a country's ability to influence others through non-coercive means, such as culture, values, diplomacy and ideas, rather than military or economic force. It is the art of seducing and convincing in order to promote one's interests on the international stage.

Artificial intelligences :

Artificial intelligences (AI) are computer systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence, such as understanding language, solving problems, learning from experience and making decisions. In other words, they are machines that mimic certain human abilities to perform specific tasks.

Jazz :

Jazz is a musical genre born in the United States in the early 20th century, characterised by improvisation, syncopated rhythms and African and European influences. It blends elements of blues, ragtime and classical music, creating a rich and dynamic form of expression.

Billboard Hot 100 :

The Billboard Hot 100 is a weekly ranking of the 100 most popular songs in the United States, based on singles sales, radio airplay and streaming. Created in 1958, it reflects musical trends and the impact of songs on the American public.

CinemaScope :

CinemaScope is a cinematographic process that uses anamorphosis to create wide-format images. This involves compressing the image when shooting in order to de-anamorphose it when projecting, allowing a typical aspect ratio of 2.39:1. Developed in the 1950s, this process was popularised by 20th Century Fox and has become synonymous with widescreen films, offering an immersive visual experience.

Steadicam :

The Steadicam is a portable camera stabilisation system designed by Garrett Brown in the 1970s. It enables smooth, stable shots to be taken, even when moving, by isolating the camera from vibrations and jolts. The device uses a harness, articulated arm and balancing mechanism to ensure image stability, facilitating movements such as dollies and pans without the need for rails or other heavy equipment. The Steadicam has been used extensively in iconic films such as "Rocky" and "The Shining", transforming the way filmmakers capture movement.
